# Smartcuts: How Hackers, Innovators, and Icons Accelerate Success

**Author**: Shane Snow | **Publisher**: HarperBusiness, 2014 | **Pages**: 272

> "The fastest route to success is never traditional, and the conventions we grow up with can be hacked."

---

## Core Thesis

**Smartcuts** are shortcuts with integrity: working smarter and achieving more without creating negative externalities. Unlike shortcuts (which are amoral and exploit loopholes), smartcuts create genuine value through lateral thinking applied to the problem of acceleration.

---

## The 9 Smartcut Principles

### 1. Hacking the Ladder
Skip rungs by reframing the problem. Don't climb the traditional career/business ladder rung by rung. Instead, find lateral moves that bypass unnecessary steps entirely.

**Application**: Like young US presidents who skipped traditional political paths, or entrepreneurs who enter markets sideways rather than through the front door.

---

### 2. Training with Masters
Formal mentorship accelerates skill acquisition exponentially. The fastest learners seek out masters and compress decades of experience into focused apprenticeships.

**Key insight**: "The presence of a teacher didn't just make things easier. Alone, students needed to stop, process, and organize new information. A tutor did some of that work for them."

**Application**: Find someone who has already solved your specific problem and learn from their compressed experience.

---

### 3. Rapid Feedback (Failure as Fuel)
Use failure as a rapid feedback mechanism, not something to avoid. The best learning systems are those with the fastest feedback loops.

**Key insight**: Second City (improv comedy) treats every "failure" as immediate data. "Bad improvisers block action... Good improvisers ADVANCE action."

**The "Yes, And" principle**: Instead of seeing failure as a stop sign, treat it as a new piece of information that moves you forward.

**Application**: Ship fast, learn fast. Every failed attempt is data that brings you closer to the right approach.

---

### 4. Leveraging Platforms
Stand on the shoulders of existing platforms, tools, and infrastructure rather than building from scratch. Why build a rickety ladder when you can take the elevator?

**Key insight**: "The top of one ladder becomes the bottom of the next." Each platform you master becomes the foundation for bigger leaps.

**Application**: Use existing networks, tools, marketplaces, or partner ecosystems as launching pads rather than building your own from zero.

---

### 5. Catching Waves
Timing matters. Identify waves of change (technological, cultural, market shifts) and ride them. It's easier to surf a wave than to create one.

**Key insight**: "Luck is often talked about as being in the right place at the right time. But like a surfer, some people are more skilled than others at recognizing a good wave and riding it."

**Application**: Position yourself where momentum is already building. Don't fight the current; redirect it.

---

### 6. Superconnecting
Build deep, quality relationships with connectors who have outsized networks. One superconnector can replace hundreds of cold connections.

**Key insight**: It's not about collecting contacts. It's about giving value to key people who are already connected to the ecosystems you want to enter.

**Application**: Identify and nurture relationships with people who sit at the intersection of multiple networks relevant to your goals.

---

### 7. Momentum (Small Wins → Big Wins)
Break big challenges into a "parlay" of small wins. Each win builds on the last, creating compounding momentum.

**Key insight**: "Researchers call this the psychology of 'small wins.'" The BYU students traded a toothpick into a TV through 12-14 incremental trades. Each trade was easy; the cumulative result was extraordinary.

**Build a reservoir**: Before your "big break" arrives, create a deep backlog of value. Sal Khan had 1,000 math lessons before Bill Gates discovered him. Michelle Phan had years of content before going viral.

**The Barbell Strategy** (from Nassim Taleb): Simultaneously be super conservative (keep the lights on) AND super aggressive (pursue the 10X opportunity). Never be in the middle.

**Application**: Don't wait for your big moment. Start stacking small wins now while building a reservoir of value that makes you ready when the wave comes.

---

### 8. Simplicity (Strip Away the Unnecessary)
"Like great writers, innovators have the fortitude to cut the adverbs." Hack your life by eliminating decision fatigue and focusing relentlessly.

**Key insight**: Steve Jobs wore identical outfits. Obama said: "I don't want to make decisions about what I'm eating or wearing. Because I have too many other decisions to make."

**Application**: Simplify ruthlessly. Fewer choices = more energy for what matters. Creativity comes easier within constraints.

---

### 9. 10X Thinking (High-Hanging Fruit)
Paradoxically, 10X goals are often easier to accomplish than 10% goals. Big causes attract big talent, big investors, and big support. They force lateral thinking.

**Key insight**: "Incremental progress depends on working harder. More resources, more effort. 10X progress is built on bravery and creativity instead. Working smarter."

**Elon Musk** didn't try to make rockets 10% cheaper. He aimed to make them 10X cheaper, which forced completely new thinking and attracted world-class engineers.

**Application**: What would 10X look like? That question alone changes the nature of the solutions you'll consider.

## Quick Reference: Which Smartcut for Which Problem?

| Situation | Primary Smartcut | Supporting Smartcuts |
|---|---|---|
| No traction in a new market | **Leveraging Platforms** | Catching Waves, Superconnecting |
| Slow skill acquisition | **Training with Masters** | Rapid Feedback |
| Stuck in incremental growth | **10X Thinking** | Hacking the Ladder |
| Can't afford to fail | **Momentum (Small Wins)** | Simplicity |
| Complex problem with no clear path | **Hacking the Ladder** | Strip Away, Rapid Feedback |
| Need credibility fast | **Superconnecting** | Training with Masters, Momentum |
| Overwhelmed / decision fatigue | **Simplicity** | Strip Away |
| Waiting for "the right time" | **Catching Waves** | Momentum (Build the Reservoir) |
| Playing it safe | **10X Thinking** | Hacking the Ladder |
