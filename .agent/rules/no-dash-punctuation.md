# No-Dash Punctuation Rule

## Scope
All workspaces. All content types. All files generated or edited by the agent.

## Rule
Systematically replace em-dashes ("\u2014") with a period (".") to start a new sentence, or a comma (",") to continue the sentence. Use no em-dashes or en-dashes as sentence punctuation. Do not swap them in where a colon, comma, period, or parentheses would do the job. If you feel an em-dash coming on, replace it with a colon, split the sentence, or use parentheses.

## Allowed Uses
- Hyphens are fine for compound words and modifiers (e.g., "well-known," "self-aware").
- Minus signs are fine for math.
- En-dashes are fine for ranges and spans (e.g., "2019\u20132021," "pages 5\u20137," "Apr 06\u201310").

## Examples

**Correct:**
- "Collaborative: building a circle of trusted people to solve the problem together."
- "He pushed the timeline again."
- "Results (good and bad) shift daily."

**Incorrect:**
- "Collaborative \u2014 building a circle of trusted people to solve the problem together."
- "He pushed the timeline again \u2014 this time without warning."
- "Results \u2014 good and bad \u2014 shift daily."

## Enforcement
- Apply during content pack generation, Slack message drafting, governance file editing, and any other text output.
- When editing existing files, scan for and replace all em-dash sentence punctuation.
- This rule overrides any copywriter voice model that would use em-dashes for stylistic purposes.
