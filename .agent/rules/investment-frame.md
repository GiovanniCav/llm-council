# Investment Frame Rule

> **Scope:** All content (internal and external), all workspaces, all clients.
> **Rule ID:** UG-008 in `.agent/governance/universal_guardrails.md`.

---

## The rule

Always use "investment" when describing pricing. Never use "cost," "price," or "fee."

## Why

The word choice matters in sales and sales-adjacent contexts. "Cost" implies expense; "investment" implies return. This is a standard frame across high-ticket consulting, coaching, and service work, and Giovanni applies it consistently across all clients.

## Where this applies

This is not limited to client-facing sales copy. The rule applies in:

- Decision memos
- Sales call scripts and notes
- Internal comms (Slack, email) that discuss money
- Strategy docs where pricing or package value is discussed
- AI-generated session summaries that reference what a client paid

## Where audience-facing content is still silent on pricing

Audience-facing content (social posts, emails to lists, blog content, etc.) does not mention prices at all per UG-001. The investment frame rule applies when pricing IS discussed, not as an override to UG-001.

## Examples

**Banned:**
- "The cost of the program is..."
- "The price for this is..."
- "There's a $500 fee."

**Correct:**
- "The investment for this program is..."
- "The investment is $500."
- "For the full package, the investment is..."

## Exceptions

None by default. If a specific context requires "cost" or "price" (e.g., direct quote from a third party, legal document, technical cost-accounting report), log the exception in the session log with the reason.
