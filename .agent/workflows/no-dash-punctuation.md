# No-Dash Punctuation Rule

## The Rule
Systematically replace em-dashes ("—") with a dot (".") to start a new sentence, or a comma (",") to continue the sentence. Use no em dashes or en dashes as sentence punctuation. Do not swap them in where a colon, comma, period, or parentheses would do the job. If you feel an em dash coming on, replace it with a colon, split the sentence, or use parentheses.

## Exceptions (Dashes Are Fine For)
- **Hyphens** in compound words and modifiers (e.g., "high-status," "pre-revenue")
- **Minus signs** in math
- **Ranges and spans** (e.g., 2019–2021, pages 5–7)

## Replacement Decision Tree
1. **Introducing a list or explanation?** → Use a colon.
2. **Starting a new thought?** → Use a period. Start a new sentence.
3. **Continuing the same thought?** → Use a comma.
4. **Inserting an aside?** → Use parentheses.
5. **None of the above?** → Restructure the sentence.

## Examples (Correct)
- "Collaborative: building a circle of trusted people to solve the problem together."
- "He pushed the timeline again."
- "Results (good and bad) shift daily."
- "I need to evaluate the opportunity properly. I don't take equity conversations lightly."
- "Send me Dustin's summary: ICPs, GTM plan, pricing."

## Examples (Incorrect)
- ~~"Collaborative — building a circle of trusted people to solve the problem together."~~
- ~~"He pushed the timeline — again."~~
- ~~"Results — good and bad — shift daily."~~

## Scope
Apply to **all writing** from Giovanni or his clients. This includes:
- Emails
- Proposals and SOWs
- Client-facing documents
- Internal strategy docs
- Social media copy
- Any artifact generated by Antigravity
